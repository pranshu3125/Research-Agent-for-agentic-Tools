Executive Summary

What a reviewer should understand in roughly two minutes.

100 apps were researched from the assignment inventory and summarized into one evidence-linked readiness view.

46 apps look buildable today, while 33 more are technically reachable with normal implementation constraints.

16 apps are primarily blocked by partner approval, enterprise setup, or commercial gating rather than missing APIs.

oauth2 is the dominant auth pattern, which means delegated user auth is the default integration shape for this portfolio.

The most common blocker is: Developer docs are public, but customer org setup, org permissions, and admin approval slow practical onboarding.

The strongest easy-win categories are the ones with public docs, self-serve credentials, and broad REST or GraphQL coverage.

Headline Insights

Portfolio-level findings before app-by-app exploration.

Productivity and Project Management is the cleanest quick-win category in this scan, driven by public docs and self-serve developer onboarding.

Finance and Fintech is the most access-constrained category, where business approval or platform policy is often the blocker rather than raw API absence.

oauth2 is the dominant auth pattern across the inventory, reinforcing that delegated user authorization is the normal case for agent toolkits.

46 apps look like immediate build candidates, while 33 more are buildable with real but manageable implementation constraints.

16 apps likely need outreach because approval, enterprise setup, or commercial access gating is the real blocker.

11 apps remain low-confidence or partially unclear after real-cache research and should get human review before roadmap decisions.

Official MCP support is rare (2 apps), while community or unofficial MCP signal exists for 12 apps and should be treated as directional rather than production-ready.
